# Verified Knot

Verified Knot is a visual philosophy of governed connection: separate systems meet only through a deliberate threshold. Form is built from dense, confident masses and a precise negative-space gate, so the composition reads first as a black-and-white mark before any color is allowed to speak. The work must feel meticulously crafted, reduced through many passes until every curve, counterform, and interval appears inevitable.

Space carries the meaning. The mark should feel like two operational bodies connecting across a controlled passage: capability and policy, agent and human approval, input and artifact. The central void is not decoration; it is the test, the audit point, the proof that a connection was allowed. Master-level execution is required here: the smallest version must remain legible, and the largest version must reward scrutiny.

Color is restrained and procedural. Obsidian gives authority, ivory gives air, charcoal supports structure, and coral appears only as the verification signature. Coral must never become the logo's crutch. If the color is removed, the symbol still works; if the color returns, it sharpens the idea rather than changing it. This requires painstaking attention to contrast, balance, and optical correction.

Rhythm comes from mirrored force, not symmetry for its own sake. The two halves interlock with enough tension to feel engineered, but enough warmth to avoid cold automation. The visual language should suggest structured AI operations, portable capability packages, policy gates, artifacts, traces, and human review without drawing literal robots, brains, sparkles, or generic network nodes.

Typography stays secondary and disciplined. The wordmark is quiet, compact, and modern, letting the symbol carry recognition. Text appears as a label, not an explanation. The final work should look like it took countless hours of refinement by someone at the top of their field: crisp edges, controlled spacing, flawless containment, and a logo system that can live as a favicon, GitHub avatar, product badge, footer mark, and open-source seal.
